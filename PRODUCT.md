# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Mix of recruiters, hiring managers, peers, and startup founders/community members evaluating Jeremy Kamber's capability, taste, and track record as a full-stack developer and product manager. They land wanting to quickly assess technical depth, product thinking, and the calibre of work — without wading through filler.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio and blog for Jeremy Kamber. Showcases selected projects, case studies, and writing to build credibility, demonstrate both technical and product skill, and open the door to opportunities — hiring, collaboration, or investment. Success is a visitor leaving with a clear, strong impression of who Jeremy is and what he builds.

## Brand Personality

Minimal, Sharp, Confident. The site communicates through restraint: less ornament, more signal. It never tries to impress with flash — it earns trust through clarity and precision. Professional without being corporate. Technical without being cold. There's a quiet confidence in letting the work occupy the foreground.

## Anti-references

- **Generic dev-portfolio templates**: No avatar hero, no skill bars, no gradient sections, no "tech stack" icons grid, no typing animations, no "Hi, I'm..." intro cards.
- **SaaS/cluttered UI**: No dashboard-like interfaces, no sidebars, no heavy tooling aesthetics, no card grids that feel like a product catalogue.
- Anything that makes the site feel templated, borrowed, or designed by committee. If someone could name the framework/library/theme it's built on at a glance, it's failed.

## Design Principles

1. **Signal over decoration.** Every visual element earns its place. No decorative flourishes, no stock imagery, no gradients, no ornamental borders. If it doesn't serve the content or the interaction, it doesn't exist.

2. **Let the work speak.** Projects and writing are the hero. The UI is a transparent pane — minimal chrome, no competing visual weight. The visitor should notice the work, not the container.

3. **Surgical precision.** Every pixel, spacing decision, and typographic choice is intentional. Nothing is approximate. Swiss precision in layout — alignment, rhythm, proportion — applied with a light touch.

4. **High contrast, high legibility.** The B&W palette is a constraint that forces clarity. No grey-on-grey ambiguity, no low-contrast body text. Information hierarchy is established through weight, scale, and whitespace — not colour.

5. **Content-first architecture.** Every page starts with the content it exists to serve, then wraps the minimum interface around it. Navigation is brief. Features are secondary. The structure follows the story.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA+ where practical, especially contrast ratios. The strict B&W palette inherently supports high contrast. Ensure keyboard navigation, focus indicators, screen reader support, and reduced-motion accommodations throughout. The grain texture overlay must respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
